1. What is disc syndrome?

The intervertebral discs are protective shock-absorbing pads between the bones of the spine (vertebrae). The discs in the neck and low back can bulge or rupture over time due to wear and tear and pinch the nearby nerve roots.

If a disc pinches the nerve in the neck, it could potentially cause pain in the neck. Symptoms such as pain, numbness, or weakness might also affect the shoulders, arms, and hands. If a disc problem occurs in the low back, pain and other symptoms might present in the low back and radiation into the hip, legs, and feet.
